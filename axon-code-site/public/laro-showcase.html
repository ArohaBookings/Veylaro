Why a compact model punches this far above its weight

It's not just weights. It's a system that checks itself.

Most local models are a raw brain and nothing else. Laro wraps its weights in a verification system that catches mistakes before you ever see them — which is how it reaches frontier-class results at a fraction of the size.

It doesn't make things up

Every API, flag, and fact is checked against ground truth before it reaches you. If Laro can't verify something, it says so instead of bluffing. Confident answers you can actually trust.

It checks its work before you see it

Laro drafts several solutions, tests each one against a strict rubric, and only shows you the winner. You get the verified answer, not the first guess.

No knowledge cutoff

Pulls live facts on demand for versions, releases, and anything time-sensitive — so it stays current long after it was built, without ever phoning home your code.

It knows your codebase

Reads your whole project on-device and grounds its answers in your real files and conventions — not a generic guess. Point it at a file or a folder and it stays in scope.

Sealed and private by design

The weights are closed and locked inside the app — nobody can extract, copy, or tamper with the model. And because it all runs locally, your prompts and code physically never leave the machine. Privacy isn't a policy here; it's physics.
